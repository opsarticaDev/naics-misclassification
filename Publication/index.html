Methodology

This study applied an automated 8-gate validation pipeline to 1,455,348 OSHA 300A injury and illness records spanning calendar years 2021–2024, downloaded from the OSHA Injury Tracking Application (ITA) public dataset. Records were flagged for potential NAICS misclassification based on code validity, multi-establishment consistency, name–code alignment, and statistical anomalies in employee counts and injury rates. Flagged records were deduplicated to unique EIN–NAICS pairs and triaged through a 14-tier classification system incorporating keyword matching, web scraping (via SearXNG), SEC EDGAR filings, SAM.gov registrations, known-company enrichment, BLS DART rate materiality assessment, and description-based contradiction detection. The pipeline achieved a 96.6% auto-resolution rate, with 2,987 records (1.8%) requiring human review and 2,591 records (1.6%) having insufficient data for resolution.

A stratified validation sample of 500 CY 2023 records was independently reviewed, yielding a population-weighted reported NAICS accuracy of 90.9%, pipeline recall of 94.9% (4 false negatives), and precision of 21.1% (by design - the pipeline is a screening tool with 97.3% auto-resolution). Cross-year persistence analysis identified 382 EINs with chronic downward DART-rate shifts and 7 EINs filing placeholder code 999999 across multiple years. Workers' compensation premium impact analysis mapped misclassification patterns to WCIRB advisory rates, finding 27.7% of cross-sector misclassifications associated with premium underpayment.
